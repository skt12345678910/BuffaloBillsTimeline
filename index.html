Buffalo Bills

A Franchise Timeline

AFL Years (1960–1969)

The Buffalo Bills were founded as a charter member of the AFL and quickly rose to dominance mid-decade, capturing back-to-back championships.

  • 1960: Franchise established
    • Original AFL team based in Buffalo, NY
    • Part of the new competing football league
  • 1962: Lou Saban becomes head coach
    • Introduced strong defensive identity
    • Helped rebuild struggling roster
  • 1964: AFL Championship victory
    • Defeated San Diego Chargers
    • Elite defense leads the way
  • 1965: Back-to-back AFL Champions
    • Established dynasty status in AFL
    • Led by QB Jack Kemp
  • Late 60s: Defensive dominance era
    • One of AFL’s best defensive units
    • Transition period before NFL merger

Early NFL Struggles (1970–1985)

After the merger, the Bills struggled for consistency but had standout performances from legendary players.

  • 1970: AFL-NFL merger
    • Bills join AFC East division
    • Face tougher NFL competition
  • 1973: O.J. Simpson 2,003 rushing yards
    • First player to break 2,000 yards
    • Achieved in just 14 games
  • 1974: Playoff appearance
    • Strong running game season
    • Short-lived success period
  • Late 70s struggles
    • Poor offensive consistency
    • Multiple losing seasons
  • 1980: Division title resurgence
    • Led by QB Joe Ferguson
    • Brief return to competitiveness

The Golden Era (1986–1999)

The Bills became one of the NFL’s most dominant teams, reaching four straight Super Bowls behind a revolutionary offense.

  • 1986: Jim Kelly joins team
    • Franchise quarterback arrives
    • Leads long-term rebuild
  • 1988: AFC Championship appearance
    • Signals rise to contender status
    • Strong core roster forms
  • 1990–1993: Four straight Super Bowls
    • Only team to do this consecutively
    • Lost all four appearances
  • K-Gun offense era
    • No-huddle fast-paced offense
    • One of NFL’s most explosive systems
  • Defensive dominance
    • Bruce Smith anchors defense
    • Multiple Hall of Fame players

Playoff Drought (2000–2016)

A long stretch of inconsistency defined this era, with frequent coaching changes and missed playoff opportunities.

  • 1999: Music City Miracle
    • Controversial playoff loss
    • Start of long drought
  • 2002: Drew Bledsoe era begins
    • Former Patriots QB joins team
    • Early offensive improvements
  • 2004: Strong defensive unit
    • Top-ranked defense in NFL
    • Still missed playoffs
  • 2015: Rex Ryan hired
    • Defensive-minded coach
    • High expectations unmet
  • 17-year playoff drought
    • Longest active drought at time
    • Frustrating era for fans

Modern Bills Era (2017–Present)

The Bills returned to contention with a new leadership core and one of the league’s most dynamic quarterbacks.

  • 2017: Playoff drought ends
    • First playoff berth since 1999
    • Emotional return to relevance
  • 2018: Josh Allen drafted
    • Becomes franchise QB
    • Develops into elite talent
  • 2020: AFC East Champions
    • First division title in 25 years
    • Deep playoff run
  • 2021–2025: Continued success
    • Consistent playoff appearances
    • 4 playoff losses to Chiefs in 5 years
  • Super Bowl contention era
    • Top offense in NFL
    • Led by Sean McDermott
